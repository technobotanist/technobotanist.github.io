Who We Are

MTR Magic Key is a research and interactive design studio based in Raleigh, North Carolina.

Founded by four SUNY Geneseo alumni from the Class of 2012: Colin Keenan (Biology), Ben Peterson (Biochemistry), Julie Cook (History/International Relations), and Alex Belensz (Geography).

All four alumni founders credit Geneseo's liberal arts training, peer networking, and undergraduate research for the skills that underpin our work.

What We Do

We design and build interactive exhibits, software, and media systems for museums and other public-facing institutions (as well as private clients) working at the intersection of science, culture, and technology.

Our work focuses on helping visitors and audiences:

  • Understand complex scientific and environmental stories
  • Engage directly with real data, live research, and place-based narratives
  • Experience spaces in new ways using contemporary tools in software, media, and AI

Selected Projects

1. Dueling Dinosaurs Camera Interactive and Fossil Lab Tools

Client: North Carolina Museum of Natural Sciences, Raleigh NC

Role:

Designed and implemented the core public-facing interactives for the Dueling Dinosaurs fossil laboratory, including:

  • A camera-based interactive that streams live fossil preparation work to gallery visitors and online audiences
  • A hands-on fossil excavation game that introduces visitors to methods and challenges of paleontological field work
  • A kiosk dedicated to viewing computer-generated videos of various potential evidence-based interpretations of the Dueling Dinosaur fossils and visitor voting on their suspected best interpretations

Impact: These tools are now the primary way the public encounters research on the Dueling Dinosaurs fossil pair, which has been covered by outlets such as the New York Times and in a recent Nature cover article on the identification of an adult Nanotyrannus.

2. Light Field Displays for Native Grassland and Park Restoration

Partners: Dix Park Conservancy and artist in residence Preston Montague, Raleigh NC

Role:

Took still imagery and design materials from a public art and ecology residency focused on native grasses and long-term park restoration and translated them into depth-embedded light field displays.

Impact: Visitors see layered views of canopy, understory, paths, and meadows in a glasses-free format, which supports intuitive understanding of ecological structure, erosion control, and circulation through restored landscapes.

3. Urban Transit and Public Space Media Installations

Example: Bus shelter art installation, Downtown Raleigh

Role:

Design and implementation support for a bus shelter art installation in downtown Raleigh that combines botanical illustration, public transit infrastructure, and neighborhood identity.

Impact: Demonstrates how small-scale interventions in public space can connect daily movement through a city with local ecology and cultural memory.
